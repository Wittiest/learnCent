learnCent

We are learnCent, an open source project
creating a tutoring platform built on the nCent blockchain.

Our platform uses recursive incentives to leverage the personal networks of students to aid other students in finding tutors. Students will refer one another for tutoring positions and receive compensation (the incentive) for those referrals

The Tutor Problem

At universities, students will often have generic tutoring available by tutors who don't specialize in their course. They may just have one class TA who will be hounded with questions. learnCent endeavors to involve students in the search for tutors, to solve this problem by connecting the students who specialize in a topic or course with the students who need aid.

Technologies

Our Express backend used PostgreSQL and Sequelize for database management. Sequelize allowed for rapid prototyping of models using it's sync methodology rather than using migrations.

Our team used React and Redux on the frontend to create a modern UI. This allows for a one-page application with a state that can be mapped to different components for easy access to different data while respecting React's one-way data flow principle

nCent's Software Developer Kit and Sandbox environment allowed for emulation of the API they will be publishing. This issues and stores data about user wallets and stamped tokens

Tutors / Students

We provide a platform for students to choose from a series of tutors and students to send a request for a tutor

User Authentification

Students and Tutors create accounts, providing details on their education, expertise, and needs. They connect with friends who are potential tutors or students who need tutoring.

Students Requests

Users can log in and see the students requesting them for tutoring. They can choose to accept, reject, or pass this student on to another tutor. They receive a reward if they refer a tutor that matches the student!

Tutors Display

Students see a display of users who could potentially tutor them or refer a good tutor. They begin a request for a tutor which will travel through network with referrals until a tutor is found.
